Structural macroeconomic forecasting using a text-based measure of policy uncertainty

This study employs a text-based uncertainty indicator within a Bayesian VAR framework to conduct robust structural macroeconomic forecasting. Our analysis utilizes monthly US data on Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU), Industrial Production, Consumption, and Employment spanning the period from 1985 to 2019. Subsequently, we construct structural forecasts by assuming a predetermined trajectory for EPU, starting from March 2023. To account for the inherent uncertainty surrounding the model parameters, we propose a Bayesian estimation method employing independent Normal-Wishart priors.

Spatial interpolation of apartment prices using Airbnb web scraped dataset

The purpose of this article is to to compare the results of several interpolation methods for spatial data of Airbnb apartment prices in Barcelona. Applying text mining techniques, an Airbnb web scraped dataset from 2022 is developed, and different interpolation methods are applied, including Voronoi approach, Nearest Neighbors (NN), Inverse Distance Weighting (IDW), and ensemble methods. Main findings Based on the RMSE value, the NN interpolation method with n = 5 yields much more accurate prediction values than the Voronoi or IDW methods.
